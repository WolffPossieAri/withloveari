Albóndiga soup is one of those comforting meals that feels like home in a bowl. This traditional hispanic soup is filled with tender meatballs, fresh vegetables, and a rich, savory broth that slowly develops flavor as it simmers.

What makes albóndiga soup so special are the meatballs themselves. Made with ground beef, rice, eggs, onion, and fresh mint, they cook directly in the broth, soaking up all the wonderful flavors of the soup while adding richness to every spoonful.

This recipe is simple, hearty, and full of nourishing ingredients, perfect for chilly evenings, family dinners, or whenever you want a warm and satisfying homemade meal.

Albóndiga Soup

Ingredients:

Soup:

1 tablespoon oil

¾ large onion, chopped

2 tomatoes, chopped

2 celery stalks, chopped

2 carrots, chopped

2 potatoes, chopped

2 cloves garlic, minced

2 soup bones

4 teaspoons chicken bouillon

Salt and pepper, to taste

Albóndiga (meatballs):

1 lb ground beef

2 eggs

½ cup uncooked rice

Remaining ¼ onion, finely chopped

2 tablespoons fresh mint, minced


Directions:

Wash and chop all vegetables: onion, tomatoes, celery, carrots, and potatoes. Set them aside.

In a large stock pot, heat 1 tablespoon oil over medium heat.

Add half of the ¾ chopped onion and the tomatoes and sauté until softened and fragrant.

Stir in 4 teaspoons chicken bouillon , then season with salt and pepper to taste.

In a large mixing bowl combine:

ground beef

eggs

rice

minced mint

remaining chopped onion

Mix until well combined. Form the mixture into small bite-sized meatballs.

Add the celery and minced garlic to the soup pot and stir.

Carefully add the meatballs and soup bones to the pot.

Cover and let the soup cook on medium heat for about 30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the meatballs are cooked through.
